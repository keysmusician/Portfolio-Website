About

I'm a software engineer. I've been studying machine learning since January 2022, and more recently I've been studying digital signal processing. In my last project, I built a generative audio model to produce audio samples which would mimic the training data. Most of my programming experience is with Python, but I'm also comfortable with JavaScript and C/C++. As a recent side project, I built a web extension that helps me begin school projects by scraping the file names from my assignments and creating files automatically.

Prior to studying at Holberton, I was self-employed as a piano teacher, and I produced music when I wasn't teaching. I found music composition challenging and time consuming, yet rewarding and enjoyable. I brought about a dozen songs to what I considered release-ready, and released 9 of them before studying software engineering at Holberton School.

Programming was a natural choice for me, as I had been fascinated with computers since I was a kid, and I've always liked to know how things worked under the hood. I had prior programming experience from high school, as well as from working on some personal projects.

Holberton had a lot of interesting qualities that made it an attractive choice for my education. It was not an easy program; I was challenged on a daily basis. The challenging, collaborative environment developed my ability to work with others, to persevere, and to know my own limits. I also honed soft skills that will benefit me throughout my professional career such as written and public communication skills, and collaboration skills.

Whether composing music or writing software, I appreciate being able to realize an idea. It's satisfying to create something with real impact and know that it's making the world a better place for not only myself, but hopefully many others. I don't just like to get stuff done, but to get it done right. I think my organizational skills, persistence, and attention to detail would be an asset to any team.
